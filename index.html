Learn about the Collatz Conjecture
Try inputting a positive integer (a whole number greater than 0), and see what happens.
The collatz conjecture is an unsolved mathemetical problem, hence it being a conjecture and not a theorum. It was named after Lothar Collatz, who personally worked on it. He is claimed to be the creator, although he denies being the first to work on it.(study.com) A whole, positive integer, X, is given. If it is odd, apply 3x + 1 and if it is even, apply x/2. This process is meant to be applied continuously. However, given time, the initial number so far always repeats in a cycles of 1, 2, 4, and back to 1[study]. The inssue of the conjecture is whether or not this applies to all numbers, or just the ones that have been applied so far.
